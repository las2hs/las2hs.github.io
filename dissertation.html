My dissertation, "Who Robbed the Woods: American Deforestation and Indigenous Literary Resistance, 1825-1930," examines representations of sylvan destruction in the century after the completion of the Erie Canal. The image of the fallen tree is used in myriad ways. White authors frequently point to it as the emblem of American superiority, the clear sign of the inevitability of the nation's expansion. Indigenous authors, however, interpret the fallen tree differently, using it in their work to bemoan those colonial practices or, in other cases, to meditate on the possibilities of Indigenous futurity.
