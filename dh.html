CBW
Since 2018 I have been Project Manager of Collective Biographies of Women, a digital project led by PI Alison Booth. CBW is composed of an annotated bibliography of women's biographies (mostly from 1800-1920) and the various projects/analyses we perform on those biographies. As PM, I train and direct a team of research assistants to use XML. We perform narratological analyses via a system we call BESS: I have blogged about BESS here and written a Python script to analyze BESS available here. BESS is vital to performing what we call "mid-range reading"; a paper about this method is forthcoming in Victorian Studies.

Virginia Discourse Lab
In 2020 I began assembling a database of direct discourse in novels. Based on this database, in 2022 I started the Virginia Discourse Lab, where I lead and collaborate with several undergraduate students at UVA. The project's large-scale goal is developing scholarly research out of the database's contents, which divides direct-discourse quotes into conversations and marks them on several aspects (e.g. verb marker, adverb marker, speaker). Currently we are working on a paper about conversations in Jane Austen's novels.

Demographic Profiling in Wikipedia
Since 2022 I have collaborated with PI Lane Rasberry at the UVA School of Data Science on various projects analyzing Wikipedia and Wikidata. Specifically, we have drafted a paper concerning the ethics of demographic profiling on the websites: how they categorize and list (potentially sensitive) information about human beings.
