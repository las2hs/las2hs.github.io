I study American literature, mostly from the long nineteenth century, focusing on Indigenous American literatures, American realism, and environmental humanities. I am a Postdoctoral Associate in English at Yale University, where I'll begin as a tenure-track Assistant Professor in 2024. My work appears or is forthcoming in ELH, Early American Literature, J19: The Journal of Nineteenth-Century Americanists, Poe Studies, Studies in American Naturalism, and Studies in American Indian Literatures.



My book project relates depictions of woodcutting in American letters to questions of Indigenous property, political agency, and environmental ethics as they develop throughout the nineteenth century and into the twentieth.



Prior to my graduate program, I earned a bachelor's degree in computer science from Brown University and worked as a software developer. An article of mine about digital "mid-range reading" has appeared in Victorian Studies.



I got my PhD at UVA. If you're a student or scholar at Yale—or anywhere—please reach out. I'd love to collaborate, talk, give advice, or generally chat.
