About me

I am currently a graduate student in English Literature, Language, and Research at the University of Virginia. I study American literature, especially Indigenous American literature and environmental humanities. I am also a digital humanist, currently serving as Project Manager of Collective Biographies of Women.


My dissertation is entitled "Who Robbed the Woods: American Deforestation and Indigenous Literary Resistance, 1825-1930." It relates depictions of woodcutting in American letters to questions of property, political agency, and environmental ethics as they develop throughout the nineteenth century and into the twentieth
